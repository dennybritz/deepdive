

Japanese share prices fell 1.01 percent Friday amid renewed
jitters about the prospects for the US economy, with investors
waiting for a meeting of the world's top finance ministers,
dealers said.


The Tokyo Stock Exchange's Nikkei-225 index of leading shares
fell 176.47 points to close at 17,363.95. The broader Nikkei-225
index of all first-section shares closed down 20.68 points or
1.20 percent to 1,705.50 points.
