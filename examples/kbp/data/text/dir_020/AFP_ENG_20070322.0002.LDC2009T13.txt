

Pakistan on Thursday successfully test-fired a nuclear-capable
cruise missile for the third time, the military said.


The Hatf VII Babur missile has a range of 700 kilometres (435
miles) and can carry all kinds of warheads, it said in a
statement.


"The Babur... is a terrain hugging, radar avoiding cruise
missile, whose range has now been enhanced to 700 kilometres," it
said.


The test was witnessed by the chairman of Pakistan's Joint Chiefs
of Staff Committee General Ehsan ul Haq, as well as senior
military officials and scientists, it said.


Pakistan President Pervez Musharraf and Prime Minister Shaukat
Aziz have congratulated the scientists and engineers on this
"very important success", it said.


The first test was conducted in 2005 and a second in March last
year.
