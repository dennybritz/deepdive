

A strong 5.9-magnitude earthquake hit Japan's southern islands on
Saturday but there were no immediate reports of damage or
casualties, meteorology officials said.


The quake occurred at 4:38 am (1938 GMT) in waters near the main
island of southern Okinawa prefecture.


Its epicentre was located about 40 kilometers (24 miles) below
the seabed, Japan's meteorological agency said.


There was no danger of a tsunami, it said.
