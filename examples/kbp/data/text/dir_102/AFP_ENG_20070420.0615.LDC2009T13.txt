

A undersea earthquake of magnitude 5.6 jolted Taiwan on Saturday,
the central weather bureau said, but there were no immediate
reports of damage or casualties.


The tremor struck at 8:32 am (0032 GMT) with its epicentre 97
kilometres (60 miles) east of Orchid Island, off southern Taiwan.
It hit at a depth of 9.8 kilometres.


Taiwan, which lies near the junction of two tectonic plates, is
regularly shaken by earthquakes.
