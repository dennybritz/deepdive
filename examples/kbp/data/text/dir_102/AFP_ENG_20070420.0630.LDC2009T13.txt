

The European Union promised an aid package of 2.7 billion euros
(3.7 billion dollars) over the next six years for development in
Latin America, including 2.8 billion in loans, officials said.


"We have designated a package of 2.7 billion euros for our
assistance to the region between 2007 and 2013," EU External
Relations Commissioner Benita Ferrero-Waldner told reporters at
the close of a meeting between the Rio Group and the European
Union.


Ferrero-Waldner said that the money would be directed to
"promoting regional integreation, especially interconnectivity."
