

Hong Kong share prices opened 0.43 percent lower Thursday after
Federal Reserve chairman Ben Bernanke said inflation remains a
threat to the US economy, dampening hopes for an early interest
rate cut, dealers said.


The market was also weighed down by rising oil prices due to
Middle East tensions after Iran captured a number of British
military personnel.


Blue chips China Mobile and HSBC faced additional pressure ahead
of the settlement of March futures contracts later in the day.
