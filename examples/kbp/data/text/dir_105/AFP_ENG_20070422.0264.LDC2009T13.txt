

Spaniard Rafael Nadal extended his all-time win streak to 67
matches at the expense of Roger Federer as he won the Monte Carlo
Masters for the third straight year here on Sunday.


The Spanish world number two won 6-4, 6-4 as he defeated his
Swiss rival for the second year in succession for the title.
Federer saved two match points before falling in one hour, 35
minutes.


Nadal improved to 7-3 over Federer, who lost three major clay
finals to the Spaniard last season, at Monte Carlo, Rome and
Roland Garros. Nadal stands 5-0 against Federer on clay.
