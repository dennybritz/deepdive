

Monta Ellis of the Golden State Warriors edged Kevin Martin of
the Sacramento Kings to become the National Basketball
Association's Most Improved Player on Thursday.


Ellis received 352 points, including 47 first-place votes from a
panel of writers. Martin picked up 349 points, including 44
first-place votes.


It was the closest finish in the history of the award since
1990-91 when Scott Skiles of Orlando edged Kevin Gamble of Boston
by four votes.
