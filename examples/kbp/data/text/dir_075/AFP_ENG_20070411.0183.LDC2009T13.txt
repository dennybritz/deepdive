

Slovakia's foreign trade deficit fell to 2.0 billion koruna (59.5
million euros, 79.78 million dollars) in February compared with
10.4 billion koruna a year earlier, the Slovak Statistics Office
announced on Wednesday.


Exports rose in February by 27.4 percent to 108.2 billion koruna,
outpacing a 15.7-percent rise in imports to 110.3 billion koruna,
the office added.


January's trade surplus was at the same time revised slightly
upwards to 4.881 billion koruna from the previously announced
3.982 billion koruna.
