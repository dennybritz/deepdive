

Friday saw the first listing of a Chinese company on the
Frankfurt stock exchange, the operator of the exhange, Deutsche
Boerse, said.


The manufacturing company Gongyou Machines, which was founded in
2004, floated shares worth 4.5 million euros (5.9 million
dollars) on the exchange.


"We hape that this is sign that other Chinese companies will
follow," Deutsche Boerse said.


The Frankfurt stock exchange earlier this month agreed to sign a
cooperation deal with the stock exchange of Tianjin.


After failing to take over or merge with major European exchanges
in recent years, Deutsche Boerse has been looking to expand in
Asia.
