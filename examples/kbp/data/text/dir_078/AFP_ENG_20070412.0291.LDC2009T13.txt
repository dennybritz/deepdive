

China's M2 broad measure of money supply jumped 17.3 percent in
March, compared with 17.8 percent in February, the central bank
said in a statement Thursday.


The narrow measure of money supply, M1, was up 19.8 percent
against 21 percent at the end of February, the bank said.


M2 covers cash in circulation plus all deposits, while M1 covers
cash in circulation and corporate demand deposits.
