

Irish 12-month inflation jumped to 5.1 percent in March, official
data showed on Thursday.


March's reading compared with a rate of 4.8 percent in February,
according to figures released by the Central Statistics Office
(CSO).


In January annual inflation had increased to 5.2 percent, the
highest rate since June, 2001,


The consumer price index (CPI) rose by 0.7 percent in March from
February, compared with a monthly increase of 0.4 percent in
March 2006.


The key monthly price changes in March were a 2.1-percent
increase in the price of clothing and footwear and a 1.6-percent
increase in transport prices.


Twelve-month inflation for services was 9.3 percent in March,
while the price of manufactured goods increased by 0.4 percent.


The EU Harmonised Index of Consumer Prices (HICP) increased by
0.7 percent in March, compared to an increase of 0.3 percent in
March 2006.


Inflation over 12 months, as measured by the HICP, increased to
2.9 percent in March from 2.6 percent in February.
