

Bulgarian consumer prices fell by 0.1 percent in March from the
figure in February but rose by 4.1 percent on a 12-month basis,
preliminary data from the National Statistical Institute showed
on Thursday.


Food prices fell in March by 0.4 percent with prices down on
vegetables by 5.8 percent, tobacco products by 4.9 percent, pork
by 3.3 percent, and soft drinks by 2.4 percent.


Prices went up on eggs by 10.3 percent, rice by 3.3 percent,
coffee by 1.7 percent, and sugar by 1.4 percent.


Bulgarian inflation stood at 6.5 percent in end-2006 but the
government is targeting to lower annual inflation to 4.4 percent
this year.
