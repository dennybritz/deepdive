

Turkey on Thursday strongly denounced the suicide bombings in
Algiers claimed by Al-Qaeda which killed 24 people and injured
more than 200 others.


"Turkey condemns the terrorist attacks which targeted innocent
people in Algeria and reiterates its call to lead a joint
struggle against terrorism," a statement from the foreign
ministry said.


In 2003, Mainly Muslim, secular Turkey was targeted by homegrown
Al-Qaeda militants who bombed Jewish and British interests in
Istanbul, killing 63 people and injuring some 600 others.
