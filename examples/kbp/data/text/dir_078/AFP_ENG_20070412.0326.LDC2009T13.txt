

Latvian airline airBaltic on Thursday announced it had launched a
new service connecting the Lithuanian capital Vilnius with
Brussels.


The carrier said it would fly to the Belgian and European Union
capital every day except Saturday, and that it aimed to draw not
only business travellers but also tourists.


airBaltic has been spreading its wings to cater to an
increasingly affluent clientele from its home region.


The company operates flights on 18 destinations from Vilnius and
46 from the Latvian capital Riga.


Founded in 1995, it is 52.6 percent owned by the Latvian state.
The Nordic carrier Scandinavian Air Services holds 47.2 percent.
