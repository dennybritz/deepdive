

Japanese swimmer Sachiko Yamada collapsed Thursday after
completing her 800-metres women's freestyle heat and had to be
helped out of the Rod Laver Arena in a wheelchair.


She finished seventh in her heat in 8 minutes 49.45 seconds and
fainted after she pulled herself out of the pool.


Onlookers rushed to help and she was seen wiping her face with a
towel as she was wheeled away.


Yamada, 24, is competing in her fourth world championships.


She qualified 18th fastest overall to miss out on a place in the
semi-finals.
