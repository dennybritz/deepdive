

South Korean President Roh Moo-Hyun on Thursday replaced four of
his ministers handling tourism, maritime matters, government
legislation and veterans' affairs, his office said.


Roh named Kim Jong-Min, head of the state-run tourism agency, as
culture and tourism minister to suceed Kim Myung-Gon, it said.


The president also appointed Kang Moo-Hyun, former vice minister
of the maritime and fisheries ministry, as minister to replace
Kim Sung-Jin.


Vice ministers Nam Ki-Myoung and Kim Jung-Bok were promoted to
ministers for government legislation and veterans affairs
respectively.


Roh steps down in February 2008 after his five-year single term.
