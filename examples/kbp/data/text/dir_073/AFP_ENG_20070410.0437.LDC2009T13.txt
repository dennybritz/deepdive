

A man who hijacked a Turkish airliner on Tuesday and forced it to
land in Ankara gave himself up to the authorities, television
channels reported.


The plane was flying between Istanbul and the mainly Kurdish city
of Diyarbakir in southeast Turkey when it was taken over, Turkish
television reported.


The jet was operated by Pegasus airlines.


The hijacker was a Turk named Mehmet Gokcingol, a transport
ministry official, Ibrahim Sahin, was quoted as saying by Turkish
media.
