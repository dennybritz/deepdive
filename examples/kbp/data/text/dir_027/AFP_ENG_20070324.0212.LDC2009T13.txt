

Australia captain Ricky Ponting on Saturday became the seventh
player to make 10,000 runs in one-day cricket.


Ponting reach the landmark when he hit a six off Andrew Hall over
long on in the World Cup clash against South Africa at Warner
Park here.


India's Sachin Tendulkar leads the group with 14,847 runs from
384 one-dayers ahead of Inzamam-ul-Haq (11,739), Sanath
Jayasuriya (11,675), Sourav Ganguly (10,632), Brian Lara (10,217)
and Rahul Dravid (10,125)


Ponting went into the game on 9,992 runs in 272 appearances.
