

World oil prices hit their highest levels of the year on Friday,
as the market was rattled by news that 15 sailors from a British
warship had been seized by Iranian naval vessels.


In London, the price of Brent North Sea crude for May delivery
surged to 63.68 dollars per barrel -- last seen on December 8,
2006 -- before pulling back slightly.


New York's main oil futures contract, light sweet crude for
delivery in May, leapt to 62.65 dollars per barrel, which was
last witnessed on December 22.
