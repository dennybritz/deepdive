

A women died and at least 21 were injured Saturday in a roller
coaster accident at a Japanese theme park crowded with
holidaymakers, police and fire officials said.


The incident happened in the early afternoon at Expo Land in
Suita city in Osaka prefecture, police confirmed.


"A woman died in the accident," said a spokesman for the Suita
city fire department.


At least 21 people were injured, he added.


The exact details of the accident were note immediately known but
according to Jiji Press the ride tilted while it was running and
some passengers were thrown away.


Officials from the theme park could not be reached for immediate
comment.


The roller coaster can fit as many as 24 people and riders use it
standing up, instead of being seated.


The accident happened during a series of national holidays called
Golden Week.
