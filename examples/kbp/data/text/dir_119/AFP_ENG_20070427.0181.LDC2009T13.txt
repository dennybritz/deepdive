

Infineon, the German semi-conductors giant, on Friday announced a
fiscal second- quarter loss of 11 million euros (14 million
dollars) compared with 26 million euros a year earlier and blamed
restructuring costs.


The results fell below anaylsts' expectations and in mid-morning
trading, Infineon stock had slid 0.92 percent to 11.79 euros on a
market trading down 0.17 percent.


Infineon, Europe's second-biggest manufacturer of semi-
conductors, recorded sales of 1.96 billion euros, down 1.6
percent.


It was the eighth quarterly loss in nine quarters for the
Bavaria-based company.
