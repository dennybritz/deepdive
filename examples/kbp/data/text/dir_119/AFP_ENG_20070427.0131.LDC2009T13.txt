

Co-host Indonesia have lined up three friendlies in Jakarta as
part of their warm-up for July's Asian Cup finals.


They will play Jordan on June 1, Singapore two days later and the
United Arab Emirates on June 30, the All Indonesia Football
Federation said on its website.


All games will be at the Gelora Bung Karno Stadium, where the
final of the Asian Cup will be held on July 29.


The website added that negotiations were under way for more
friendlies, against Uruguay, Mexico and Jamaica.


At the Asian Cup, Indonesia are pitted in Group D along with
South Korea, Bahrain and Saudi Arabia.


The finals begin in Bangkok on July 7.
