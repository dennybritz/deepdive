

British publisher Pearson said Friday it had agreed to buy two
education publishing businesses from Dutch rival Reed Elsevier
for 950 million dollars (700 million euros) in cash.


"Pearson, the international education and information company,
today announces that it has agreed to acquire Harcourt Assessment
and Harcourt Education International," a statement said.


"The acquisition extends Pearson's position as the world's
leading education company by adding international reach,
complementary products and new capabilities," it added.
