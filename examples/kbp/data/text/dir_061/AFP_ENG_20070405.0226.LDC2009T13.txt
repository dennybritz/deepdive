

A suicide bomber blew up a dump truck outside the office of a
television channel owned by Iraq's leading Sunni political party
on Thursday, a security official said.


Ten Baghdad TV employees were wounded in the attack outside their
office at in the capital's western Al-Jamiyah district, he said.


The 24-hour channel is owned by the Iraqi Islamic Party, a
leading Sunni party in Iraq. One of its leaders, Sunni moderate
Tareq al-Hashimi is one of Iraq's two vice presidents.


The security official said local police sealed off the district
after the blast and the channel briefly stopped transmiting
programmes.
