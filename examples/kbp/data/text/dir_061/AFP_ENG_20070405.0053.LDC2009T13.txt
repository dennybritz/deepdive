

Japanese share prices closed down 0.30 percent on Thursday as
investors locked in profits after two days of strong gains,
turning cautious on the eve of key US jobs data, dealers said.


The Tokyo Stock Exchange's Nikkei-225 index of leading shares
fell 52.67 points to 17,491.42. The TOPIX index of all issues on
the Tokyo Stock Exchange's first section shed 9.80 points or 0.57
percent to 1,720.72.
