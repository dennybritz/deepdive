

Hong Kong share prices finished the Friday morning session 0.53
percent higher, extending gains as investors cheered Wall
Street's further push into record territory overnight, dealers
said.


Property stocks drew follow-through interest on hopes that a
government land auction on Tuesday will attract strong bids from
major developers, they said.


PetroChina jumped on news of a large oil find in northeastern
Bohai Bay off Tianjin, helping provide additional support.


The Hang Seng Index ended the morning up 110.47 points at
20,792.05, off a high of 20,883.21 and a low of 20,714.82.


The property sub-index was up 693.44 points or 2.74 percent at
25,955.52.
