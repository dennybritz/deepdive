

Japanese share prices fell 0.96 percent in morning trade
Wednesday as investors fretted over the health of the US economy
and upcoming domestic corporate results, dealers said.


They said that investors were unnerved by a weaker dollar
following a sharp plunge in existing US home sales.


The Tokyo Stock Exchange's Nikkei-225 index of leading shares
fell 167.18 points to 17,284.59 by the lunch break.


The broader Topix index of all first-section shares dropped 17.54
points or 1.03 percent to 1,688.62.


Decliners beat gainers 1,158 to 425, with 146 stocks flat.


Volume was 878 million shares, up from 794 million on Tuesday
morning.


Hiroichi Nishi, an equities official at Nikko Cordial Securities,
said: "Speculators apparently took advantage of the lack of
strong buying interest in the broader market and sold off the
index futures in particular."


Many investors moved to the sidelines to monitor earnings
announcements and to await the Golden Week holidays that start
here this weekend, he said.


KDDI ended the morning 50,000 yen or 5.05 percent lower at
941,000 despite reporting that its operating profit rose 16.2
percent in the last fiscal year.


Yahoo Japan shot up by 34,000 yen or 9.25 percent to 40,150 after
posting a record net profit in the fiscal fourth quarter.


Canon added 80 yen or 1.2 percent to 6,740 after the high-tech
giant reported a 21-percent rise in net profit in the first
quarter of this year and increased its forecasts for 2007 as a
whole.
