

Chinese police have issued arrest warrants for two employees of
the Agricultural Bank of China suspected of stealing 51 million
yuan (6.6 million dollars), state press said Tuesday.


Police were searching for Ren Xiaofeng, 33, and Ma Xiangjing, 36,
both from a branch in Gandan city in northern China's Hebei
province, Xinhua news agency said.


The two were employed at the bank's cash administration centre in
Gandan, it said.


The two were seen fleeing the bank in a van shortly after it was
discovered that 51 million yuan was missing on Saturday, the
report said.


Police are offering a 100,000 yuan reward for any information
leading to the the arrests of the two suspects, it added.
