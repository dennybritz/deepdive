

Pope Benedict XVI on Tuesday sent a message of condolence over
the Virginia Tech shooting massacre that claimed 33 lives, saying
he was "deeply saddened" by the "senseless tragedy."


The pope wished to "convey the assurance of his heartfelt prayers
for the victims, their families and for the entire school
community," Vatican Secretary of State Cardinale Tarcisio Bertone
said in a communique.


"In the aftermath of this senseless tragedy he asks God our
Father to console all those who mourn and to grant them that
spiritual strength which triumphs over violence by the power of
hope and reconciling love," the pope's telegram said in English.


The cable was addressed to Richmond Bishop Francis Xavier
DiLorenzo, whose diocese includes Blacksburg, Virginia, site of
the university.
