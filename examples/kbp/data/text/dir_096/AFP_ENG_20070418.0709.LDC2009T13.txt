

Three southern French towns were rocked Wednesday by small
explosions but there were no casualties, police said.


The blasts occurred at 9:00 pm in Ciboure, Guethary and Saint-
Jean-de-Luz near the town of Pau.


A flat under construction was destroyed, a private building
damaged and the windows of a real estate agency's office were
shattered, police said.


They did not reveal the nature of the explosives used or the
motive behind the coordinated low-level blasts.


Two low-level explosions occured last week in nthe towns of
Bayonne and Biarritz, both located in Basque country.
