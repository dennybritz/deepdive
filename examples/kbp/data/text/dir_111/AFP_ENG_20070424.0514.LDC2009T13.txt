

At least six dissidents were set free Tuesday after serving
prison terms of almost two years each for defying the communist
Cuban government, dissident sources said.


Elizardo Sanchez, a dissident spokesman who leads the Commission
for Human Rights and National Reconciliation, announced the
releases of Manuel Perez Soria, 55; Lazaro Alonso Roman, 32; and
Emilio Leyva, 42.


Another dissident group, the outlawed National Coordinator for
Past and Current Political Prisoners, added to the list three
more disidents who were released: Duylian Ramirez, Elio Chavez
and Jose Diaz Silva. There ages were not immediately given.
