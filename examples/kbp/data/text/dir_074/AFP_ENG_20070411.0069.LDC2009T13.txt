

Czech industrial production rose by 15.4 percent in February on a
12-month comparison after an increase of 9.8 percent in January,
the Czech Statistical Office announced on Wednesday.


In February, output climbed by 1.9 percent from the January
figure. In January production rose by 2.3 percent compared with
December.


Electrical and optical equipment production jumped by 41.2
percent over the year with output from the much bigger transport
equipment sector rising 16.4 percent, the office said.


Czech industrial output rose by 9.7 percent in 2006, after
growing by 5.7 percent in 2005, the highest figure since 2001.
