

Ireland's trade surplus increased by 5.3 percent to 2.439 billion
euros (3.329 billion dollars) in February from January, official
data showed Tuesday.


The data released by the Central Statistics Office (CSO) gave
final figures for January and first estimates for February.


Exports increased month-on-month in February by 1.0 percent to
7.889 billion euros and imports showed little change at 5.451
billion euros.


The trade surplus widened by 14 percent in January from December,
according to the CSO.


The January figures showed that imports from Britain rose by 9.0
percent from a year earlier and from the United States by 75
percent. Imports from Japan were down 19 percent.
