

Two Afghans and two foreigners were killed when a roadside bomb
blast ripped through a United Nations vehicle in the southern
city of Kandahar, police said Tuesday.


"Two Afghans and two foreigners were killed in this explosion,"
said Sayed Agha, a police officer at the scene of the attack in
Kandahar city, adding that initial details indicated the
foreigners were Nepalese.


UN spokesman Aleem Siddique told AFP that there was a "roadside
bomb explosion against a UN convoy which was going to its base in
Kandahar" but had no details of casualties.


Kandahar is the birthplace of the fundamentalist Taliban movement
that was has waged an increasingly fierce insurgency against
foreign and government troops and aid workers since being ousted
in 2001.
