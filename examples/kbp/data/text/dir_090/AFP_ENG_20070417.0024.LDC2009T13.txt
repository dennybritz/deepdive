

Hong Kong share prices finished the Tuesday morning session 0.31
percent lower as investors locked in profits after gains of over
400 points Monday, dealers said.


China Mobile and some mainland financials saw selling pressure
but HSBC helped limit the market's losses after news that a Saudi
investor has bought a 3.1 percent stake in the global banking
giant.


The Hang Seng Index ended the morning down 63.32 points at
20,694.21, off a low of 20,635.63 and a high of 20,816.11.
