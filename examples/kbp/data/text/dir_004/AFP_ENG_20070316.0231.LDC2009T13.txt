

Former Scotland forward Adam Robson, who also served as President
of the Scottish Rugby Union, has died at the age of 78.


Born in the Scots rugby stronghold of Hawick, Robson won 22 caps
for his country between 1954 and 1960.


An art teacher by profession, he was the SRU president in
1983-84, a season in which Scotland won the Six Nations Grand
Slam.
