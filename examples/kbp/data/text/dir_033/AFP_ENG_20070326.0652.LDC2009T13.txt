

South Korea was the world's 11th largest exporting nation in
2006, one place higher than the previous year, a report said
Tuesday.


The Korea International Trade Association said in the report that
exports were worth 326 billion dollars last year, an all-time
high despite the strength of the won against the dollar and yen.


Imports were worth 309 billion dollars last year, the 13th
largest figure in the world.


Germany was the world's largest exporter with 1.11 trillion
dollars in 2006, followed by the United States with 1.04
trillion, China with 969 billion dollars and Japan with 647
billion, according to the report.
