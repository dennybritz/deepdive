

Russian oil company Rosneft Tuesday bought 9.44 percent of its
shares that had been owned by stricken giant Yukos, paying 7.6
billion dollars (5.7 billion euros) for a package of assets at an
auction.


The package, which also included promissory notes for the Yukos
subsidiary Yuganskneftegaz, was sold after bidding opened at a
starting price equal to 7.44 billion dollars (5.6 billion euros).
