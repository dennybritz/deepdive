

Slovenian consumer prices increased by 1.1 percent in March from
February and by 2.6 percent on a 12-month basis, official data
showed Friday.


The largest increase of prices was registered in the clothing and
footwear sectors as well as in housing and food, Slovenia's
Statistical Office reported.


In February, consumer prices fell by 0.1 percent from January but
rose by 2.3 percent on a 12-month basis, according to the EU
harmonised index of consumer prices.


Slovenia adopted the euro on January 1, in a changeover the
European Commission, the European Central Bank and Slovenian
authorities consider successful.
