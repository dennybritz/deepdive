

The northern Norwegian city of Tromsoe was unveiled Friday as
candidate for the host city for the 2018 Winter Olympics,
sporting authorities announced here.


Tromsoe, which has 65,000 inhabitants and is plunged into total
darkness for a large part of winter, beat off competition from
Trondheim in the west and a joint bid from Oslo and Lillehammer.


The candidature, made by the Norway's sporting federation and
Olympic committee, must now receive final backing from the
Norwegian government.


Norway has twiced hosted the Winter Games, in 1952 in Oslo and in
1994 in Lillehammer.
