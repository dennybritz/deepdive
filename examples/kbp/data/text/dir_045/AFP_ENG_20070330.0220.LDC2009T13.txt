

German electrical engineering giant Siemens said Friday it had
won a contract worth 550 million euros (731 million dollars) to
buld a gas-fired power plant in the Netherlands.


The contract for a 870-megawatt plant in the Zeeland province was
awarded by Delta Energy of the Netherlands and Electricite de
France, it said in a statement


Siemens will supply two gas turbines, two steam turbines, two
generators and a range of other equipment to the facility, which
was scheduled to go online in the spring of 2009, it said.
