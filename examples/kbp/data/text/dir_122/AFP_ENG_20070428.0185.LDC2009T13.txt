

German Chancellor Angela Merkel Saturday called for both Russia
and Estonia to show restraint in the row over the removal of a
monument to Soviet soldiers from Tallinn city centre.


"Angela Merkel today telephoned (Estonian) Prime Minister Andrus
Ansip and (Russian) President Vladimir Putin," a statement from
the German government said.


"The chancellor called for restraint and urged them not escalate
the situation in any way," the statement said.
