

Japan's vice minister for foreign affairs, Midori Matsushima,
launched a visit to Cuba Wednesday to discuss bilateral
relations, media reported.


Matsushima was to meet with Cuban Foreign Minister Felipe Perez
Roque and other officials Wednesday. The length of her trip was
not revealed.


The vice minister is to visit medical facilities related to
Operacion Milagro, a Cuban-Venezuelan program for people with
vision problems.


Matsushima hopes to review bilateral relations and topics of
interests to Japan.
