

Public support for French President Jacques Chirac who recently
announced his retirement from politics increased in March,
according to a newspaper poll Saturday.


Support for Chirac, who was head of state for 12 years, jumped
three points from last month to 46 percent, with 53 percent
saying they were dissatisfied, according to the IFOP poll for Le
Journal Du Dimanche.


Dominique de Villepin, a loyal supporter of the president, also
saw a rise in popularity to 40 percent, an increase of two points
on the February poll.


Over half, 58 percent, said they were unhappy with the prime
minister, a drop of two points on last month.


The poll was conducted by telephone between March 8 and 16, with
1,935 people questioned.
