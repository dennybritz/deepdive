

Hong Kong share prices finished the Thursday morning session 0.45
percent lower as renewed worries over interest rates prompted
investors to lock in profits on recent gains, dealers said.


They said US Federal Reserve meeting minutes released overnight
indicated that policymakers remain concerned over inflation,
leaving the door open for interest rate hikes.


The news led to to falls on Wall Street and in regional markets
such as Tokyo.


The Hang Seng Index ended the morning down 92.69 points at
20,356.74, off a low of 20,293.33 and a high of 20,428.60.
