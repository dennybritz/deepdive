

An earthquake measuring 5.0 on the Richter scale shook southern
Bulgaria on Thursday without causing any injuries or damage, the
country's seismology center said.


The tremor hit at in the early afternoon some 320 kilometers
south of the capital Sofia.


It was felt in the southern town of Kardzhali but there were no
reports of injuries or serious damage, the national civil
protection service said.


The seismology center had recorded another tremor late Wednesday,
measuring 2.7 on the Richter scale.
