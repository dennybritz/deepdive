

Fifteen people have died and seven others reported missing
following flash floods in Algeria, state television reported
Sunday.


The deaths occurred on Friday and Saturday in the M'sila region,
240 kilometres (150 miles) south of the capital Alger, it said.


State radio had on Saturday put the toll at nine and reported two
children missing. It said several heads of cattle were missing
while many homes had been washed away.


In March, some 15 people died after torrential rains in the
country's north.
