

Japan's Takefumi Sakata became the new World Boxing Association
flyweight champion, beating title-holder Lorenzo Parra of
Venezuela with a technical knockout victory on Monday.


The bout ended abruptly when Parra did not stand up to fight at
the beginning of the third round, forcing the referee to declare
a technical knockout. The knockout time was 14 seconds into the
third round.


It was the 27-year-old Japanese fighter's fourth attempt at a
world title. Sakata, ranked third in the WBA, stretched his
record to 30 wins, including 15 KOs, against four defeats and a
draw.


It was the first defeat for Parra, who has 27 wins, including 27
KOs.
