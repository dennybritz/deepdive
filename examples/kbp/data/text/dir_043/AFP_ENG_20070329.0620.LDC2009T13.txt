

Alois Kankovsky of the Czech Republic won the men's omnium crown
held for the first time at the world track cycling championships
here on Thursday.


Kankovsky claimed a total of 19 points to secure his first world
title ahead of Argentina's Walter Perez, who had 28 points.


Charles Bradley Huff took the bronze medal with a total of 37.


The omnium is composed of five races: a 200m flying start time
trial, a 5km scratch race, an individual pursuit over 3km, a 15km
points race and a kilometre time trial.


Despite being introduced at world level it is not on the Olympic
progamme for Beijing in 2008.
