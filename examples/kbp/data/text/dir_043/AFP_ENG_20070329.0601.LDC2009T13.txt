

New Zealand were 37-2 after 10 overs in their World Cup Super
Eights match against West Indies at the Sir Vivian Richards
Stadium here on Thursday chasing 178 to win.


Skipper Stephen Fleming was on 20 and Scott Styris was on 0.


Daren Powell took both the wickets to fall, bowling Peter Fulton
for a second- ball duck before having Hamish Marshall, who only
arrived in the Caribbean two days ago, caught by Brian Lara at
mid-off for 15.


Earlier, the West Indies, who lost their opening Super Eights
match to Australia by 103 runs on Wednesday, were bowled out for
177 in 44.4 overs with Jacob Oram, Shane Bond and Daniel Vettori
all taking three wickets each.
