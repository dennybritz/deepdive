

US President George W. Bush on Friday discussed the Israeli-
Palestinian conflict with Saudi Arabia's King Abdullah and
Egyptian President Hosni Mubarak, White House spokesman Tony Snow
said.


Bush and the Saudi ruler "discussed the effort to advance toward
a Palestinian state and also peace between Israelis and
Palestinians," Snow told reporters.


They also "talked about Iran, the situation in Lebanon, and also
the president thanked the king for Saudi Arabia's participation
in the recent conference in Iraq," the spokesman said.


Bush also thanked Mubarak for Egypt's taking part in the Iraq
conference, and discussed "the situation in Iraq, the Israeli-
Palestinian situation, Lebanon, and a forthcoming trip to the
region by Secretary of State Condoleezza Rice," said Snow.
