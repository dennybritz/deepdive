

An Afghan army and police operation to clear  Taliban strongholds
in southern Afghanistan has left 69 militants and seven police
dead, the defence ministry said Friday, raising an earlier toll.


"Sixty-nine enemy elements were killed," defence ministry
spokesman Mohammad Zahir Azimi told reporters in Kabul of the
operation in Helmand province Thursday.


"Forty-nine bodies of the enemy were left at the battleground.
Seventeen enemy elements were arrested, including three wounded,"
Azimi said.


"Seven police were martyred," and 19 men from the police force,
army and the intelligence service were wounded, he said.


Officials said earlier Friday that more than 40 Taliban and seven
police were killed.
