

The Swedish maker of speciality steel SSAB said Thursday it
planned to buy Canadian group IPSCO for 7.7 billion dollars (5.6
billion euros).


In a statement it said the friendly takeover had been approved by
IPSCO management.


On April 12 IPSCO was valued at about 6.13 billion dollars (4.56
billion euros) on the New York Stock Exchange and the company
last year produced about four million tonnes of steel.


It reported a net profit of 643 million dollars.


SSAB shares were suspended on the Stockholm stock exchange in the
late morning pending the announcement.
