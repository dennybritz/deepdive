

Three men were arrested on Thursday in connection with the July
7, 2005 attacks on London transport that killed 56, including the
four suicide bombers, the Metropolitan Police said.


Two men, aged 23 and 30, were arrested shortly before 1:00 pm
(1300 GMT) at Manchester Airport, north-west England, as they
were about to board a flight to Pakistan, a statement said.


A third, aged 26, was arrested at a house in Leeds, northern
England, shortly after 4:00 pm (1600 GMT). All were detained on
suspicion of the "commission, preparation or instigation of acts
of terrorism", police said.
