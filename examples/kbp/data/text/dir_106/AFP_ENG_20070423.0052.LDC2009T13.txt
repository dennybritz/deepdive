

Israeli security forces were placed on maximum alert on Monday as
the Jewish state marked Memorial Day for soldiers killed and
prepared to celebrate Independence Day, the army said.


The army sealed off the Palestinian territories on Saturday night
ahead of the Israeli holidays.


At sunset on Sunday, Israel began to mark Memorial Day in honour
of soldiers killed in wars and civilians killed in attacks. At
sunset on Monday, the Jewish state will begin to mark the 59th
anniversary since its creation in 1948.


Both holidays are marked each year according to the Hebrew
calendar.
