

A trapeze artist fell 40 feet to his death in front of horrified
spectators during a circus performance in California, officials
said Tuesday.


Roberto Valenzuela, 35, died on Monday after falling head first
onto the ground with no net to break his fall during a display in
Whittier, just outside Los Angeles, police said.


Valenzuela was pronounced dead at the scene after being treated
by Los Angeles Fire Department paramedics who were on hand for
the following act, which involved fire, a police spokeswoman
said.


Police are investigating the accident, the spokeswoman added.
