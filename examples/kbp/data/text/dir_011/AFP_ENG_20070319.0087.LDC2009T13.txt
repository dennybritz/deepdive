

Gunmen killed three civilians in broad daylight on Monday in the
mainly Shiite provincial capital of Hilla, south of Baghdad, a
police officer said.


Gunmen opened fire in three different areas of the city, also
wounding another four people, said police Lieutenant Haider al-
Lami.


In another attack in Babil province, a woman and her four-year-
old daughter were wounded when insurgents fired two mortar rounds
on a residential area of the town of Iskandiriyah.


Police in Hilla also found large caches of weapons, including 500
mortar rounds and artillery shells.


The body of an unidentified man was also found in the town of
Mahaweel, north of Hilla, police said.
