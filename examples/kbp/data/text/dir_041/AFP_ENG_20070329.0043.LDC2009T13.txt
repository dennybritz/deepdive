

A liquefied petroleum gas tank exploded in a luxury hotel in
southern Turkey on Thursday, killing one employee and injuring
ten other people, media reports said.


Foreign tourists and hotel employees were among those injured in
the explosion in the kitchen of the hotel in the resort of Belek,
near the Mediterranean city of Antalya, the CNN-Turk news channel
said.


The Anatolia news agency identified the victim as the hotel's 32
-year-old chief butcher.


Local paramilitary troops, which oversee security in Belek,
confirmed the explosion but refused to give details.


The cause of the blast was not immediately clear.


Belek is one of the most popular holiday destinations on Turkey's
southern coast, atracting thousands of foreign and domestic
tourists every year.
